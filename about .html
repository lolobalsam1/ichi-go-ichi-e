the site

The window

Third floor, N25, mechanical engineering side.

Most people in this building never come to this corridor. It belongs to the mechanical engineering students. The industrial design side rarely wanders here. The hallway is quieter than the ones below, utilitarian, unremarkable. And at the end of it, facing out, there is a window.

From up here you see rooftops: flat concrete slabs, vents, the geometry of the campus seen from above. A few trees break the pattern. Students pass in the distance, small below. The sky takes up more space than it does on the ground.

Nobody designed this view to be looked at. But it is there, every day, changing with the light and the season, waiting for someone to stop.

Hanging at the window: a pair of headphones. Attached to an mp3 player with songs already loaded, chosen for this view, this light, this corridor that most people never enter. Each file is named after a lyric from the song, not its title. You scroll through and read fragments of feeling before you even press play. Instrumentals are named after the feeling they left. You just have to put them on.

This corridor has a protocol. People move through it with purpose: to the lab, to a class, between buildings. It is a connector, not a place. The window is incidental to that movement. Nobody stops.

The gesture asks: what if someone stopped?

musubi · 結び

the red string

結び · the red string of fate

In Japanese belief, an invisible red thread connects people who are meant to meet, tied at the finger, stretching across distance and time, unbreakable.

The red string posted near this QR code is not decoration. It is an instruction: follow it, to the third floor, along the mechanical engineering corridor, to the window at the end. There you will find headphones hanging. An mp3 player. Songs already chosen for this exact view.

Put them on. Look out. Stay for one song.

You were not meant to be here by accident. Something brought you.

in place · 設置

Installation

The string left the window, crossed the sill, and ran down the corridor floor to meet whoever happened to be walking by.

the thinking

The four questions

01

What is the protocol of this site?

The third-floor N25 mechanical engineering corridor is transitional and semi-exclusive. Industrial design students rarely enter. Those who do move fast. The window at the end of the hall is incidental to everyone's path. Nobody has a reason to stop there. The view of rooftops, trees, and sky goes largely unseen.

02

What did I want to change or reveal?

That this window has a view worth stopping for, and that music can make a place feel intentional. Everyone in this corridor is already listening to something privately. The gesture makes one shared soundtrack for this window, and asks: what does it feel like to look out from here with a song chosen for exactly this moment?

03

Why this addition and not something else?

Headphones and an mp3 player are physically modest but experientially total. They don't alter the space. They alter the person standing in it. Each song is named after its best lyric, so scrolling through the player is already an experience before anything plays. And the songs keep growing: anyone who scans the QR code can request a song, and approved requests are added to the player.

04

What impact did it bring?

Someone followed the string. Someone put on the headphones and stood at this window long enough to finish a song, something they would never have done otherwise. This journal is where they, and everyone after them, leave a trace of that moment.
